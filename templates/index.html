About the Event

The Robotic Championship is a prestigious annual competition that has grown into one of the most anticipated events in the regional technology and engineering calendar. Year after year, it brings together the brightest minds in robotics — from ambitious first-year students taking their first steps into the world of engineering, to seasoned competitors who have spent countless hours designing, building, and refining their machines to near perfection. This championship is more than just a competition; it is a celebration of human ingenuity, creative problem-solving, and the relentless drive to push the boundaries of what technology can achieve.

At its heart, the Robotic Championship exists to do something truly meaningful — to recognize talent that might otherwise go unnoticed, and to give extraordinary individuals the chance to prove themselves on a real, competitive stage. Cash prizes are awarded annually to top-performing teams and individuals, not only as a reward for their hard work and dedication, but as an investment in their futures. These prizes serve as a launching pad, helping winners fund further research, continue their education, and pursue careers in some of the most exciting fields in the world today.

This landmark event is organized and proudly led by Georg Feil, a Professor in the Faculty of Applied Science & Technology at Sheridan College and the dedicated Head of the Sheridan Robotics Club. Georg is no ordinary academic — his career reads like a roadmap through some of the most groundbreaking technological achievements of the modern era. He has worked extensively in the aerospace and space robotics field, contributing to the development of next-generation Canadarm prototypes, a sophisticated two-armed neurosurgery robot, and rovers designed to traverse the surfaces of Mars and the Moon. He has also played a significant role in developing systems for space-based radio astronomy, including contributions to the Japanese HALCA and Russian RadioAstron missions. Since joining Sheridan in 2012, Georg has channeled this extraordinary depth of experience into inspiring the next generation of engineers and roboticists. His vision, leadership, and genuine passion for the field are what make the Robotic Championship not just an event, but a movement. Learn more about Georg at his official Sheridan faculty profile.

The Robotic Championship 2026 promises to be the biggest and most exciting edition yet. Whether you are a competitor, a supporter, or simply someone who believes in the power of technology to change the world — this is an event you will not want to miss.
